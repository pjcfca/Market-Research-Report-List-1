<p><ul><li>Mammalian Protein Expression Systems</li><li>Baculovirus Protein Expression Systems</li><li>Prokaryotic Protein Expression Systems</li><li>Yeast Protein Expression Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Protein expression systems refer to the method of producing proteins in a host organism for research, therapeutics, and industrial applications. Mammalian protein expression systems utilize mammalian cells for accurate protein folding and post-translational modifications. Baculovirus protein expression systems use insect cells infected with a baculovirus for high yields of complex proteins. Prokaryotic protein expression systems, such as E. coli, are commonly used for simple protein production. Yeast protein expression systems like Saccharomyces cerevisiae offer efficient protein production and eukaryotic-like post-translational modifications.</p></p>
