<p><p>Power regulators are devices used to manage and stabilize the voltage or current of power supply systems. There are two main types of power regulators: Mechanical power regulators, which use mechanical components to adjust the power output, and Intelligent power regulators, which utilize advanced technologies such as microprocessors and sensors to automatically regulate power based on real-time conditions. Mechanical regulators are simpler and more cost-effective, while Intelligent regulators offer greater precision and efficiency in managing power supply fluctuations.</p></p>
