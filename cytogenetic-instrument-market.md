<p><ul><li>Fluorescence in Situ Hybridization</li><li>Comparative Genomic Hybridization</li><li>Flow Cytometry</li><li>Multiplex-Fluorescence in Situ Hybridization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Cytogenetic Instrument market comprises various types of instruments used for genetic analysis. Fluorescence in Situ Hybridization (FISH) is a technique that uses fluorescent probes to detect specific DNA sequences, helping researchers identify genetic abnormalities. Comparative Genomic Hybridization (CGH) allows the detection of chromosomal changes and copy number variations. Flow Cytometry enables the analysis of cells based on their size, shape, and DNA content. Multiplex-Fluorescence in Situ Hybridization combines FISH with other techniques to analyze multiple genetic targets simultaneously. These instruments aid in understanding genetic disorders and conducting research in the field of genetics.</p></p>
