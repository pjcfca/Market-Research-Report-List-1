<p><p>Organic soil conditioners can be classified into two main types: natural and synthetic. Natural soil conditioners are derived from organic materials such as compost, manure, and peat moss. They work by improving soil structure, increasing microbial activity, and providing essential nutrients to plants. On the other hand, synthetic soil conditioners are manufactured products that are typically composed of chemical compounds. While they can also improve soil fertility and structure, some synthetic soil conditioners may have negative environmental impacts and lack the benefits of natural alternatives.</p></p>
