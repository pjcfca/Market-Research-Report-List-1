<p><p>Leavening agents are substances used in baking to help dough or batter rise by releasing gases that expand during the baking process. Inorganic leavening agents, such as baking powder, contain chemicals like sodium bicarbonate. Organic leavening agents, like cream of tartar, are derived from natural sources. Biological leavening agents, such as yeast, are living organisms that produce carbon dioxide gas as they feed on sugars present in the dough. Each type of leavening agent has unique properties that affect the texture and flavor of the baked goods.</p></p>
