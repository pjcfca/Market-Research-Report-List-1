In the network security market, private blockchain refers to a closed system accessible only by authorized individuals or organizations, offering enhanced privacy and control. Public blockchain, on the other hand, is open to everyone and provides transparency and immutability. Lastly, consortium blockchain involves multiple organizations jointly managing and validating transactions, offering a balance between privacy and transparency. These distinct market types cater to various security needs while harnessing the benefits of blockchain technology.</p></p>
