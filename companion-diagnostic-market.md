<p><p>Companion diagnostic is a branch of personalized medicine that involves identifying patients who will benefit from a specific therapy. Immunohistochemistry (IHC) is a companion diagnostic market type where antibodies are used to detect specific proteins in tissue samples. It helps determine if a patient's disease is associated with the presence or absence of specific proteins. Molecular diagnosis is another companion diagnostic market type that involves analyzing genetic material (DNA or RNA) to detect mutations or alterations that may impact treatment decisions. It provides insights into a patient's genomic profile to guide targeted therapies.</p></p>
