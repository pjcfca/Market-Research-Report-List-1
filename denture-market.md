<p><p>Dentures are removable replacements for missing teeth and their surrounding tissues. They are typically made of materials like acrylic resin or metal, and they serve as an option for those who have lost their teeth due to age, disease, or injury. Dentures can improve the appearance of a person's smile, as well as restore their ability to eat and speak properly.</p><p>The global denture market has experienced significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue to expand at a CAGR of 8.3% during the forecast period. Several key factors are contributing to this growth. Firstly, the rising geriatric population, along with an increasing prevalence of dental diseases and tooth loss, is driving the demand for dentures. Additionally, growing awareness about oral healthcare and the availability of advanced denture products are further propelling market growth.</p><p>Another emerging trend in the denture market is the adoption of digital dentistry technologies. Digital dentures are designed using computer-aided design/computer-aided manufacturing (CAD/CAM) systems, which enable more precise and customized denture solutions. This technology offers benefits such as reduced chair-time, improved accuracy, and higher patient satisfaction.</p><p>Furthermore, the preference for implant-supported dentures is growing rapidly. These dentures are secured in place by dental implants, providing superior stability and improved chewing efficiency compared to traditional removable dentures. The increasing adoption of dental implants, along with advancements in implant materials and techniques, is boosting the demand for implant-supported dentures.</p><p>Overall, the denture market is witnessing significant growth driven by factors like the growing geriatric population, increasing dental diseases, advancements in digital dentistry, and the rising popularity of implant-supported dentures. These trends, along with technological advancements and increasing disposable incomes, are expected to continue to fuel market growth in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><p>The denture market consists of two main types: full denture and partial denture. Full dentures are designed for individuals who have lost all of their natural teeth. They are crafted to replace a complete set of teeth and are supported by the underlying gum tissue. On the other hand, partial dentures are recommended for patients who have only a few missing teeth. They are customized to fit in with the remaining natural teeth and are held in place by clasps or attachments. Both types aim to restore oral functionality and enhance the aesthetic appearance of the individuals.</p></p>
