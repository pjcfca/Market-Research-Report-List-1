<p><p>Unfractionated heparin (UFH) is a naturally occurring anticoagulant drug that is widely used for the prevention and treatment of blood clotting disorders. It is derived from porcine intestinal mucosa or bovine lung tissue and is composed of a complex mixture of polysaccharide chains. UFH works by inhibiting the activity of clotting factors and preventing the formation of blood clots.</p><p>The Unfractionated Heparin Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to factors such as the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and thromboembolic disorders, increasing surgical procedures, and the growing geriatric population. Additionally, the demand for UFH is also driven by its cost-effectiveness compared to alternative anticoagulant therapies.</p><p>A key trend in the Unfractionated Heparin Market is the development of low molecular weight heparins (LMWH) as an alternative to UFH. LMWHs offer a more predictable anticoagulant response and can be administered once or twice daily, making them more convenient for patients. However, UFH still holds a significant market share due to its wide availability and lower cost.</p><p>Geographically, North America is expected to dominate the Unfractionated Heparin Market during the forecast period.
