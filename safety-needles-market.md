<p><p>Safety needles are designed to reduce the risk of needlestick injuries for healthcare professionals. Active safety needles have a built-in mechanism that requires user activation to engage the safety feature. They can be further categorized as semi-automatic or fully automatic, depending on the level of user involvement required. Passive safety needles, on the other hand, automatically activate the safety feature without additional user steps. Both types of safety needles play a crucial role in safeguarding healthcare workers from accidental needlestick injuries during medical procedures.</p></p>
