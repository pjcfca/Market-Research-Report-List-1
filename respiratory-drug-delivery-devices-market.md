<p><p>The respiratory drug delivery devices market includes three main types: Metered Dose Inhalers (MDIs), Dry Powder Inhalers (DPIs), and Nebulizers. MDIs are handheld devices that release a specific dose of medication in aerosol form for patients to inhale. DPIs deliver powdered medication directly to the lungs when the patient inhales forcefully. Nebulizers convert liquid medication into a fine mist that patients can inhale through a mask or mouthpiece. These devices play a crucial role in delivering respiratory drugs to patients with asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and other respiratory conditions.</p></p>
