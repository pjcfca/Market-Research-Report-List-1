Additionally, companies are increasingly focusing on sustainability and ethical sourcing practices, leading to the integration of environmental and social considerations into supply chain operations.</p><p>Another emerging trend in the SCM market is the emphasis on cloud-based solutions. As a result, there is a growing demand for supply chain solutions that can enhance agility, risk management capabilities, and supply chain visibility to mitigate disruptions.</p><p>In conclusion, the SCM market is expected to experience significant growth, driven by the adoption of advanced technologies, focus on sustainability, and the need for resilient supply chains. <p><p>Supply Chain Management (SCM) refers to the strategic coordination and control of the flow of goods, services, and information from suppliers to end customers. The market types in SCM are categorized as cloud-based, on-premise, and SaaS-based. Cloud-based SCM is a software solution hosted on a remote server, allowing companies to access and manage their supply chain activities online. On-premise SCM is a software installed on the company's own servers, providing complete control and customization but requiring maintenance. SaaS-based SCM is a software delivered through the internet, providing scalability, cost-effectiveness, and easy integration with other applications.</p></p>
