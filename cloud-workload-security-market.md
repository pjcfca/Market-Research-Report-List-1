<p><strong>The Cloud Workload Security Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Public Cloud</li><li>Private Cloud</li><li>Hybrid Cloud</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Public Cloud: This market refers to the delivery of cloud services over the internet to multiple organizations and users. It offers flexibility, scalability, and cost-effectiveness but may have lower security controls due to its shared infrastructure and multi-tenant environment.</p><p>Private Cloud: In this market, cloud services are exclusively used by a single organization, offering greater control, customization, and security. Organizations can host their own private cloud infrastructure or use third-party providers. Private clouds are best suited for industries with strict data regulations and sensitive information.</p><p>Hybrid Cloud: This market combines both public and private cloud environments. It allows organizations to leverage the benefits of both models, enabling flexibility, scalability, and the ability to move workloads between public and private clouds as needed. Hybrid clouds offer increased security through private cloud elements while utilizing the cost-efficiency and elasticity of public clouds for non-sensitive data and workloads.</p></p>
