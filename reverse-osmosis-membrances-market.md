<p><p>Reverse osmosis membranes are classified into two main types: Cellulose based membranes and thin film composite membranes. Cellulose based membranes are made from natural materials and offer high rejection rates for contaminants. On the other hand, thin film composite membranes are made from synthetic materials and have a higher flux rate. Both types of membranes are commonly used in water treatment systems to remove impurities and provide clean drinking water. The market for these membranes continues to grow as the demand for clean water increases worldwide.</p></p>
