<p><p>A percutaneous decompression system is a minimally invasive surgical technique used to relieve pressure on nerves or spinal discs. It involves the insertion of a needle or tube through the skin and into the affected area to remove or reduce the source of compression. This procedure is typically used to treat conditions such as herniated discs, spinal stenosis, and nerve root compression.</p><p>The percutaneous decompression system market is experiencing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the rising prevalence of spinal disorders and associated chronic pain is driving the demand for minimally invasive procedures. Additionally, advancements in technology, such as the development of advanced imaging techniques and surgical instruments, are improving the efficacy and safety of percutaneous decompression procedures.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing preference for outpatient procedures and shorter hospital stays is also contributing to the market growth. Percutaneous decompression systems offer several advantages over traditional open surgery, including reduced risk of infection, minimal scarring, and faster recovery times.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there is an increasing focus on product development and innovation.
