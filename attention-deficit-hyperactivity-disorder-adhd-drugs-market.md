<p><p>The Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) Drugs market can be categorized into two types - Stimulants and Non-Stimulants. Stimulants, the most commonly prescribed medications for ADHD, work by increasing dopamine and norepinephrine levels in the brain, improving focus and reducing impulsivity. These drugs include methylphenidate and amphetamines. On the other hand, Non-Stimulant medications like atomoxetine work differently, targeting norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors. They are often prescribed as an alternative when stimulants have undesirable side effects or do not produce the desired effects.</p></p>
