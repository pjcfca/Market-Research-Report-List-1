<p><p>Biological indicators for sterilization are used to assess the effectiveness of sterilization processes in healthcare facilities and industries. These indicators consist of living organisms, such as spores of bacteria and fungi, which are placed inside test packs and subjected to the same sterilization processes as the items being sterilized. If the sterilization process is effective, these spores should be killed, indicating that the process has achieved the desired level of sterilization.</p><p>The global biological indicators for sterilization market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. This growth can be attributed to factors such as the increasing emphasis on infection control measures in healthcare settings, the growing number of surgical procedures, and the rising demand for sterile pharmaceutical products in the pharmaceutical and biotechnology industries. <p><p>Biological indicators for sterilization are used to validate the effectiveness of sterilization processes in industries like healthcare and pharmaceuticals. The self-contained biological indicator is a complete system that contains a population of microorganisms specifically designed to be resistant to the sterilization method being tested. It provides a clear positive or negative result. On the other hand, the biological indicator strip is a small strip containing spores or microorganisms that can be attached to or placed inside the items being sterilized, offering a convenient and cost-effective solution for routine sterilization monitoring.</p></p>
